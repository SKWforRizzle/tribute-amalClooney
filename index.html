Here's a timeline of Amal Clooney's life:

  • 1978 - Born in Beirut, Lebanon

  • 1980 - Leaves Lebanon with her family to escape the Lebanese Civil War and settled in Buckinghamshire

  • 2000 - Graduate with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Jurisprudence from St. Hugh's College, Oxford

  • 2001-2002 - Completed a Master of Law at New York University of Law and passed the bar exam

  • 2004 - Finished her judicial clerkship at the International Court of Justice

  • 2005-2010 - Shifted her legal work towards international law, participating in the investigation of the assassination of former Lebanese prime minister Rafic Hariri and the International Criminal Tribunal for the horrific crimes committed during the Yugoslav wars

  • 2012 - Appointed Kofi Annan's senior advisor

  • 2014 - Married American actor George Clooney

  • 2016 - Co-founded the Clooney Foundation for Justice & partnered with the Aurora Humanitarian Initiative

  • 2016-Present - Using her fame to support Communities around the World

Advice from HER for young women:

"It’s the courage of the people I represent. It’s also my students. I see a generation that is not only much more politically engaged than I ever was at their age, but also one that sees themselves as agents of change. That gives me hope because they’re the ones we have to rely on in the future. To young women, I would say: Reach for the stars and believe in yourselves, and know that the only thing you can regret is not trying. A lot of success is down to luck and hard work, but it’s also down to having the courage to go for it. Even if you don’t know if you can do it. Even if you’ve never done it before. Even if you don’t know anyone who’s done it. Just shifting your thinking away from 'Why me?' and instead to 'Why not me?'”
